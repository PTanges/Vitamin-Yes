Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine)

What is Vitamin B6?

Vitamin B6, also known as pyridoxine, is a water-soluble vitamin that is naturally present in some foods,
added to others, and available as a dietary supplement.
Common sources of Vitamin B6 is found in: tuna, salmon, yellowfin, chicken breast, chickpea,
fortifed cereals, and more.

What are the benefits of vitamin B6?

Vitamin B6 reduces risk of cardiovascular diseases

Warnings:

Deficiency of B6:
Generally speaking, deficiency in vitamin B6 occurs commonly with deficiency of B9 (folic acid) and B12 (cobalamin)
Common symptoms of B6 deficiency is:
  • depression
  • confusion
  • microcytic anemia
    • Consequences of long-term deficiency:
  • kidney disease
  • intestine diseases such as celiac disease, ulcerative colitis, and Crohn's disease
  • inflammatory diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis
    • Over-consumption of B6:
  • neuropathy in the hands and feet
  • ataxia
  • nausea
